--- layout: post title: Nerd Diet is Live ---

This blog is my wife's idea. One day we were talking about my recent attempt to loose weight and I said something that made her laugh. The statement is best summed up in this tweet.

Its nice to hear when people notice that you are loosing weight. However it would be more helpful if people told me I was getting fat.

— raytiley (@raytiley) January 15, 2014

Somehow this evolved into writing a blog about loosing weight. At first I dismissed the idea, but like most ideas my wife has it ended up being too good. The idea wormed its way into my subconceous and here I am, fat, but less fat than I was and writing about it.

I've tried to loose weight before. Dieting, excecising, juicing, even fasting. Nothing seems to stick. The most weight I have ever lost is when I thru-hiked the Appalachian Trail. For those unfamiliar, the Appalachian Trail is a long walk in the mountains from Georgia to Maine. As weight loss plans go hiking a long distance trail is effective but far from pratical.

After the hiking I didn't think much about my weight. As a matter of fact I paid so little attention that since completing the trail I have gained almost one hundred pounds. That is not a typo. When I finished hiking in 2006 I weighed 214 pounds. On January 1st, 2014 I weighed 312.8 pounds.

At the time of writing this blog post I weigh 280.1 pounds. I've lost 32.7 pounds all within the past two months. I realize that is a short amount of time and that I am far from the finish line, but this time feels different.

Usually when on a weight loss kick I'm obsesing. When am I going to eat next? What am I going to eat? How can I stand being so hungry? Eating one donut is ok if I go running after right?. The theme being that loosing weight is painful, a chore that I need to endure so I can go back to eating normally.

I'm still obsessing about my weight loss, but its more enjoyable. I am obsessing about my trend, calorie breakdown, body chemistry, tools I want to code, etc. I'm geeking out over my own body and enjoying it.

What I envision this blog becoming is another tool in helping me geek out about my weight. I'll share what I am doing, what I have done and if it is working. I will probably write some software to share with others, in addition to reviewing blogs, books and software written by others. I do not expect anyone to pay attention, this is soley for my own benefit.

If you do happen to find yourself reading my blog thanks for taking the time. Please leave a comment and share your thoughts, once I get those working :)
